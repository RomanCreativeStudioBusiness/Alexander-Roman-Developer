About me

I'm Alexander Roman — a self-taught developer who ships.

I got into front-end development the way most working developers actually do: by building real things for real people, not by following along with tutorials. I run Roman Creative Studio, a small digital agency, which means I've had to learn what actually matters to a business — speed, clarity, and a site that makes visitors take action.

That experience shapes how I write front-end code. I think in terms of user flow and conversion before I think in terms of components. I'm comfortable owning a build end-to-end: structuring the HTML, writing clean CSS with a real design system, and adding just enough JavaScript to make an interface feel alive without slowing it down.

I'm now looking for a junior front-end role where I can bring that same ownership to a team — learning modern frameworks and best practices from experienced engineers while contributing UI/UX instincts sharpened on real client work.
